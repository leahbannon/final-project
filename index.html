About

Leah is a civil servant and product lead at 18F, founder of the Tech Lady Hackathons, and a San Franciscan.

This year, Leah is also a Technology and Democracy Fellow at the Harvard Kennedy School's Ash Center. She is writing recommendations for digital modernization initiatives in government.

Leah helped found 18F, a project inside the federal government working with other agencies to successfully deliver efficient, easy-to-use digital services. She is an agile coach at 18F and a product lead for the national FOIA project.

Leah has also served as a product lead for the Federal Election Commission's homepage and data redesign at fec.gov, single sign on and DACA applications for myUSCIS, and sharing government web traffic data on analytics.usa.gov. You can read more about her work in her 18F blog posts.

getting into tech

  • To learn to code, you need to practice. To start a new career, you need a portfolio and practice. Code for America brigades are awesome for that.
  • Bento.io: online tutorials and a guide for what to learn next
  • Code Academy: good for a basic intro to different languages
  • Impostor Syndrome is real! The best way that I've found to combat it is mentoring.

work & productivity

  • UK Government Digital Service Design Manual: This is how you should build things, especially software. It's an excellent intro to user-centered design and agile.

life advice I may or may not have learned yet
